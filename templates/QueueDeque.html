Queue-Deque



A queue is a data structure that adheres to the First-In-First-Out (FIFO) principle, indicating that the first element inserted into the queue will be the first one to be removed. This concept is analogous to real-world queues or lines, where the first person to join the queue is the first to receive service. The implementation of queues can vary and is adaptable to specific application requirements. Queues can be realized using diverse data structures like arrays, linked lists, or others. They find common usage in tasks such as scheduling, breadth-first search algorithms, and resource management within computer systems. Department of Computer Science, University of Michigan Tech
